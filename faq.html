Frequently Asked Questions

General Questions

What is ACS citation format?

ACS (American Chemical Society) citation format is a standardized way of citing sources in scientific papers, particularly in chemistry and related fields. It follows specific guidelines set by the American Chemical Society.

Why should I use ACS format?

ACS format is required by many scientific journals and academic institutions, particularly for chemistry-related papers. It ensures consistency and allows readers to easily locate your sources.

What types of sources can I cite?

Citation Format Questions

How do I cite multiple authors?

For multiple authors, list them separated by semicolons. For example: "Smith, J. A.; Johnson, M. B.; Williams, R. C."

What if I'm missing some information?

Try to include all required fields marked with an asterisk (*). If optional information is unavailable, you can still generate a citation with the essential elements.
