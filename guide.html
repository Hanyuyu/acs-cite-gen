ACS Citation Style Guide

Introduction to ACS Style

The American Chemical Society (ACS) style is widely used in chemistry and related sciences. This guide will help you understand how to properly format your citations and references according to ACS guidelines.

General Format Rules

  • References should be numbered in the order they appear in the text
  • Use superscript numbers in the text to indicate references
  • The reference list should be double-spaced
  • Journal abbreviations should follow Chemical Abstracts Service Source Index (CASSI)

Citation Formats

Journal Articles

Author 1; Author 2; Author 3; etc. Title of Article. Journal Abbreviation Year, Volume, Pages.

Example: Li, H.; Rothberg, L. Colorimetric Detection of DNA Sequences Based on Electrostatic Interactions with Unmodified Gold Nanoparticles. Proc. Natl. Acad. Sci. U.S.A. 2004, 101, 14036-14039.

Books

Author 1; Author 2; etc. Book Title, Edition Number; Publisher: Place of Publication, Year; Pages.

Example: Chang, R. Chemistry, 9th ed.; McGraw-Hill: New York, 2007; pp 45-48.

Websites

Author (if available). Title of Site. URL (accessed Month Day, Year).

Example: American Chemical Society. ACS Style Guide. https://www.acs.org/style-guide (accessed December 7, 2024).

Tips for In-Text Citations

  • Use superscript numbers to indicate references
  • If referring to a reference as part of a sentence, use normal text with parentheses
  • Multiple references should be separated by commas
  • References should be numbered in order of appearance
